Trips we offer

Whistler Blackcomb, Canada
Highlights: Largest ski resort in North America, with over 8,000 acres of terrain.
Why Go: Whistler has something for everyone, from beginners to pros, with numerous runs, terrain parks, and breathtaking backcountry options.
Niseko, Japan
Highlights: Renowned for its light, fluffy powder and consistent snowfall.
Why Go: Niseko offers some of the best powder in the world and unique tree runs.
Laax, Switzerland
Highlights: Known for its expansive freestyle terrain and world-class parks.
Why Go: This snowboarder's paradise has one of the best freestyle parks globally.
Hakuba Valley, Japan
Highlights: Offers multiple resorts with access to deep powder and beautiful alpine scenery.
Why Go:Known for its incredible snowfall, Hakuba provides diverse terrain with challenging backcountry routes and accessible beginner-friendly slopes
St. Anton, Austria
Highlights: Part of the expansive Arlberg ski area with reliable snowfall and extensive terrain.
Why Go: It’s famous for both its expert-level off-piste terrain and lively après-ski culture. Advanced snowboarders will enjoy the challenging descents and ample freeride options.
Jackson Hole, Wyoming, USA
Highlights: Offers challenging terrain with steep slopes and backcountry.
Why Go: Known for its rugged, natural terrain and steep descents, Jackson Hole is perfect for advanced snowboarders looking for an adrenaline rush.
Verbier, Switzerland
Highlights: Part of the 4 Valleys ski area with incredible off-piste routes.
Why Go: Verbier is known for its challenging and scenic terrain, attracting expert snowboarders and freeriders. It’s a top choice for those seeking thrilling powder runs.
Aspen, Colorado, USA
Highlights: Four mountains, each with a unique vibe and varied terrain.
Why Go: Aspen has everything from gentle beginner slopes to world-class parks and powder-filled bowls. Its vibrant town offers plenty to do off the mountain.
Queenstown, New Zealand
Highlights: Offers an excellent winter escape with Southern Hemisphere snowboarding.
Why Go: New Zealand’s winter season (June-August) makes it a great option for year-round snowboarders. Queenstown is surrounded by several ski resorts, with a beautiful landscape and adventure options.
Chamonix-Mont-Blanc, France
Highlights: Known for its challenging terrain and massive vertical drops.
Why Go: Chamonix is iconic for its off-piste and extreme routes. Experienced riders flock here for the incredible scenery and technical challenges.
